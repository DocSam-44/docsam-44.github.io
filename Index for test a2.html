One Image x 5

By: Samantha Mayer

For this version of the image, you must look to the background of the entire page. I not only assigned the image to the background, but also added an unusual red shap with slight opacity. I did this to have the image represent the manifesto and how it's ideas are consistent throughout, It also represents how the unusual image makes it confusing/convoluted to look at which is how I felt while reading it. While the ideas may have been consistant and easily recognizable, I felt as though a lot of the depth to the writing was lost on me. I feel that this unusual visual that it provides can be compared to the fight against perfection that is depicted in the manifesto as it is not meant to feel pleasing to everyone or 'fit in'.

Hear Me!

This image version has been enlarged to represent the magnitude of the concept being played with; further complex due to the time in which the manifesto was written. I also feel that the enlargement of this photo embodies how "in your face" the ideas of the text felt as a reader. To further emphasize the ideas of the text that felt very "in your face" to me, I have added the text overlay of "hear me!" because it is what the narrative felt to be asking from me while reading the text. By making the text semi-transparent, I feel that I was able to portray the message without hiding the image fully as it is still of importance for the art to be whole. Additionally, I feel that the word overlay feels very punk-movement-esk which corelates to the political aspects of the manifesto.

For this version, I decided to use the tile effect. The repetition of this image felt indicative of the repatition of ideas restated throughout the manifesto which were presented as if to be new, but instead felt exhaustingly similar to me. When you first glance at the tiled image, you could struggle to identify that it is multiple copies of the same image and instead feel that your eyes can't tell what they are looking at. This is similar to how I felt as I attempted to make sense of the reading's deeper meanings. The more I saw of the same ideas, the more my eyes glazed over as they do when viewing a repeated image.

For this version, I have adjusted the brightness, contrast, and saturation level by manipulating the filter. I have also used the mix-blend-mode to multiply 50%. These manipulations of the image are significant because the increase of contrast can be understood as a critic to the one-sidedness of perspectives at play in the manifesto. While characteristic of a manifesto, it made the reading seem overdone; just as the colours of the photo have been overdone and the once appealing picture is not easily recognizable.

The above picture remains in it's original colour to contrast the image to the left. This represents the difference between when the text was written and the present in which I am reading it. Knowing what I know from the technological advancements of the current age, offers entirely new perspectives to the reading just as having a coloured image provides and entirely new perspective to its black and white counterpart.

The above picture is black and white to represent the past in which the text was written. I also feel that the image now feels striped back like the language and descriptions in the manifesto. There was no in depth analysis that uses overly niche knowledge which allows it to be accessible. Similarily, I feel that black and white images are more accessible because while not everyone is colourblind, everyone had varied perception of colours. By stripping that aspect back, it can be more widely examined and possibly critisized by those viewing it.

By bringing the images above forward, I made sure that they are not lost under the chaos of the rest of the page. This is done to help accentuate the juxtaposition of that specific image pairing
